## The Reality

The technology is no longer the bottleneck; the tools (AlphaMissense, Nanopore, Federated Learning) exist. The challenge now is implementation and integration. The transition from the "event-based" Hackathon to the "always-on" Network is the next logical step in the evolution of precision medicine, promising a future where the "diagnostic odyssey" becomes a relic of the past.

## Strategic Recommendations

### 1. Invest in Federated Infrastructure

Hospitals and research centers should prioritize "AI-ready" edge computing infrastructure to enable participation in federated networks.

### 2. Adopt "Doctor-in-the-Loop" Training

Medical boards should recognize AI interaction and critique as a valid form of Continuing Medical Education (CME) and contribution to the field, incentivizing clinician participation in model training.

### 3. Standardize the "Tool List"

The community must develop standardized languages (like Workflow Description Language - WDL) for expressing diagnostic logic so AI agents can reliably execute it across different platforms.
